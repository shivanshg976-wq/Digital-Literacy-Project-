Title: Case Study: UPI Fraud via Fake Part-Time Job Scams

What is the crime? UPI fraud occurs when scammers manipulate victims into authorizing a Unified Payments Interface (UPI) transaction, resulting in money being instantly siphoned from the victim's bank account. A common variation targeting youth is the "Fake Part-Time Job" scam.

How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):

1. The Bait: A college student receives a WhatsApp message offering a lucrative part-time data entry job requiring no prior experience.

2. The Hook: The scammer asks the student to pay a small, refundable "registration fee" of ₹500 to secure the job.

3. The Manipulation: The scammer sends a QR code or payment link. Once the student pays the ₹500, the scammer claims there was a technical error and asks them to scan a new QR code to receive a "refund."

4. The Theft: The new QR code or link is actually a request to send money, not receive it. The scammer rushes the student, creating a false sense of urgency. The student enters their UPI PIN, thinking they are accepting a refund, but instead, thousands of rupees are debited from their account.

Who is targeted and what are the consequences?
College students and young adults are frequent targets because they are actively seeking internships and part-time income. The consequences include severe financial loss, emotional distress, and a compromised sense of digital security. Because UPI transactions are instant, recovering the funds is extremely difficult once the PIN is entered.
