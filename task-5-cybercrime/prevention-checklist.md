Title: Stay Safe Online - A Guide for College Students


1. Protect Your PIN: Never share your UPI PIN, ATM PIN, or banking passwords with anyone, including bank officials or customer care executives.


2. Receiving Money Doesn't Require a PIN: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money—only to send it.

3. Verify Links: Hover over links in emails and messages to check the actual destination URL before clicking, to avoid phishing websites.

4. Use 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all your social media, email, and banking applications.

5. Beware of Public Wi-Fi: Avoid making financial transactions or logging into sensitive accounts while connected to free public Wi-Fi networks at cafes or airports.

6. Limit Personal Info: Do not overshare personal details like your home address, daily routines, or phone number on public social media profiles.

7. Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your phone and laptop operating systems to patch security vulnerabilities against malware and ransomware.


8. Report Suspicious Activity: If you become a victim of a cybercrime, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.
